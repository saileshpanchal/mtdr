# Counterfactual and Evidence

Runs *before* acceptance, against the judgement; **evidence-review** runs against the record's
completeness. Both exist because a record can be complete and still be a rationalisation. Read
`spec-decision-assurance.md` §5 for where the output lands.

## The one rule

**If no evidence could change the answer, it was never a decision.** The case must name what would
falsify the preferred option — or say plainly that nothing would, which is itself a finding.

## Process

1. **Build the strongest alternative.** Not the strawman the decision paper rejected — the
   alternative its most capable critic would argue. If the TDR's alternatives section cannot
   survive this test, the record goes back before the case proceeds.
2. **Price doing nothing.** The honest consequences of no decision, on the same time horizon and
   with the same scrutiny applied to the preferred option. "Do nothing" framed as catastrophe is
   usually a tell.
3. **Separate assumptions from facts.** Every load-bearing claim is either evidenced (where?) or
   an assumption (stated as one, with its confidence). Assumptions dressed as facts are the
   primary mechanism of predetermined answers.
4. **Grade the evidence.** Quality, provenance and age are the **evidence-review** skill's
   discipline — apply it rather than re-deriving it here — then add the one test it does not run:
   was each item produced to *inform* the decision, or to *support* it? Name the missing or
   inaccessible evidence and what it would have settled.
5. **Test the causality.** Could the claimed benefits occur anyway, without this decision? What
   competing causal explanations exist for the evidence presented?
6. **Set the falsifiers.** What observed outcome would prove the preferred option wrong — and what
   evidence would *reopen* the decision after acceptance? Both feed the monitoring contract and
   the learning obligations directly.

## Outputs

Strongest-alternative statement · do-nothing baseline · assumptions ledger (with confidence) ·
evidence-quality assessment + missing-evidence list · competing causal explanations ·
falsification and reopening conditions (feed the monitoring contract).

## Anti-patterns

- **Strawman alternatives** — rejecting options no one would actually argue.
- **Catastrophised inaction** — pricing "do nothing" with none of the rigour applied to acting.
- **Assumption laundering** — load-bearing guesses presented in the indicative mood.
- **Advocacy evidence** — counting only the evidence gathered after the answer was chosen.
