# Adaptive Capacity

A system question, never an individual-mindset question: it does not ask whether people possess a
growth mindset — that would reduce a system property to a training need. It asks whether the
*decision* leaves the organisation able to learn. Read `spec-decision-assurance.md` §5 for where
the output lands.

## The one rule

**A decision that cannot be challenged, observed or reversed converts every wrong assumption into
permanent architecture.**

## Process

### Conditions for learning

1. **Challenge without penalty** — can people contest the decision's assumptions after acceptance,
   through a route that reaches the accountable owner?
2. **Authority to act** — does someone hold explicit authority to stop, narrow or reverse the
   decision, and is the path from evidence to that authority shorter than the harm's velocity?
3. **Near misses as evidence** — will near misses surface as monitoring signals, or be hidden as
   failure? What does the incentive structure actually reward?
4. **Frontline signal path** — can the people who see the consequences first reach the decision
   owner without translation through three layers of reporting?
5. **Capacity to act on learning** — is there budget, headroom and mandate to change the decision
   when the evidence says so, or is the review theatre?
6. **Small reversible tests** — can the decision be run as bounded experiments before full
   commitment? If not, why not — and what substitutes?
7. **Dissent retained** — are the minority and dissenting views recorded in the TDR's
   alternatives, so a future reader knows what the organisation chose to disbelieve?
8. **The mind-changing evidence** — what, concretely, would cause the organisation to change its
   mind? If nothing would, the decision is a belief, not a judgement.

### Execution capability

The same decision may be appropriate for one organisation and reckless for another. Assess
honestly: skills available; operational maturity; governance maturity; monitoring capability
(can the thresholds in the monitoring contract actually be observed?); supplier capability;
readiness of the teams who will live with it.

## Outputs

Adaptability assessment — reversibility, feedback latency, decision rights, learning triggers ·
execution-capability assessment · inputs to the learning-obligations block and the reversibility
and detectability axes of the risk vector.

## Anti-patterns

- **Mindset laundering** — turning a system deficiency into a training recommendation.
- **Reversibility fiction** — calling a decision reversible when no one holds the authority,
  budget or nerve to reverse it.
- **Review theatre** — scheduling reviews with no capacity to act on their findings.
- **Capability optimism** — assuring the decision the organisation wishes it were, not the one it is.
